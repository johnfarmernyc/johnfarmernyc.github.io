What Is A Design Sprint?

Invented by Google Ventures, a Design Sprint is a weeklong workshop for companies launching a new product or improving an existing one. It combines idea generation, rapid prototyping and user testing to create amazing user experiences.

All in one week?

Yes. Here’s How It Works.

Understand

Defining the true problem, outlining the business opportunity and exposing assumptions are critical first steps to a well designed product or service.

Stakeholders share their unique perspectives and develop a common understanding of the problems and goals.

  • Problem definition
  • Key assumptions
  • Product success definition
  • Customer identification
  • Team interviews
  • Existing user analytics

Ideate

Explore as many possible design concepts, regardless of feasibility. New solutions are uncovered when approaching the problem from unique perspectives.

  • Idea generation
  • Paper prototyping
  • User story mapping

CONVERGE

At this point there will be numerous potential design concepts and solutions, but you won’t be able to test them all. Hone in on a limited feature-set to prototype and test, ensuring key assumptions are validated or invalidated.

  • Decision making process
  • Feature prioritization

Prototype

Create a medium-fidelity prototype or landing page for user testing. This is an intense day of non-stop building and watching the product design come to life.

  • Medium-fidelity prototype
  • Landing pages

Test & Learn

Test your prototype with existing or potential users. Multiple in-depth interviews uncover insight into what works and what doesn't. This learning is critical to understanding how your users will interact with your product, before you build it.

  • User research interviews
  • Landing page tests
  • A/B tests for existing users
